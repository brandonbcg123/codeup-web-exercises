What is a national park?
A scenic or historically large area of public land set aside for native plants, animals and the places in which they live.

Can I visit a national park alone on my own?
That depends on the park, some parks require a local forest guide.

How do I obtain a park entrance pass?
You can obtain park entrance passes by visiting a park site that charges an entrance fee.

How do I reserve a tour in a park?
You can book some park tours through Recreation.gov

What was the first national park in America?
YellowStone National Park

What is the largest national park in the United States?
The Wrangell-St. ELias National Park.

How close can I get to wild animals in National Parks?
Stay at least 25 yards away from animals that mean no harm, but stay at least 100 yards away from animals with a dangerous intent.

Why do people go to national parks?
Most of the time its because people enjoy a national park's scenery and landscape

What are geysers?
A hot spring in which water intermittently boils, sending a tall column of water and steam into the air.

How many national parks are in the United States?
Ther are 58 national parks.

Parks List

Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone is the world's first national park.
  • The park sits on top of an active volcano.
  • The park has more than 500 active geysers.
  • The park has 67 species of mammals.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon is located in Arizona.
  • The park is around 6000 feet deep.
  • The Colorado River runs through this park
  • This park creates its own weather.

Glacier National Park

  • Glacier National Park was established on May 11, 1910.
  • The park has a total of 762 lakes.
  • The park has 175 mountains.
  • The park has 26 glaciers.
